Science-Informed Fueling: The P-Formance Rationale

Our approach is built on a deep understanding of carbohydrate metabolism. Key scientific insights shape our fructose-free strategy:

How P-Formance Works: The SGLT1 Pathway for Glucose/Maltodextrin

1. Ingestion: P-Formance fuel (Glucose/Maltodextrin based) is consumed.
‚Üì
2. Intestinal Lumen: Glucose molecules (from direct glucose or broken-down maltodextrin) arrive in the small intestine.
‚Üì
3. SGLT1 Transporter Activation: Glucose binds to the SGLT1 transporter on intestinal cells. This is an active process, co-transporting sodium.
‚Üì
4. Efficient Absorption: Glucose is transported into the intestinal cell. SGLT1 has a high capacity (~60-70g/hr).
‚Üì
5. Enters Bloodstream: Glucose exits the intestinal cell and enters the bloodstream directly.
‚Üì
6. Fuel for Muscles: Circulating glucose is readily available for uptake by working muscles for energy.

P-Formance optimizes this pathway for efficient, gut-friendly energy delivery, completely avoiding the complexities and potential GI issues of fructose absorption via GLUT5.

Research confirms that fructose ingested alone, or in non-optimized ratios, is linked to GI distress due to saturation of its GLUT5 transporter, leading to osmotic discomfort. This underpins our fructose-free commitment.

üîóUnderstanding MTC Strategies

Multiple Transportable Carbohydrate (MTC) formulas (glucose+fructose) aim to maximize CHO uptake. P-Formance offers a distinct alternative for those preferring to avoid fructose or whose needs align with efficient SGLT1 fueling.

üíßMaltodextrin's Role for Gut Comfort

Maltodextrin's lower sweetness and osmolality (vs. simple sugars) can improve palatability and GI tolerance, aligning with P-Formance's use of optimized maltodextrin/glucose blends for effective SGLT1 absorption and gut comfort.

By understanding these complexities, P-Formance consciously chooses a fructose-free path, aiming to provide the most reliable, gut-friendly, and effective fuel for a significant segment of the athletic population.
