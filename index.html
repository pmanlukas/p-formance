Science-Informed Fueling: The P-Formance Rationale

Our approach is built on a deep understanding of carbohydrate metabolism and the challenges athletes face. The "Fructose in Endurance Sports Nutrition" report highlights several key insights that shape our fructose-free strategy:

‚ùóFructose & GI Distress Link

The report confirms that fructose ingested alone, or in excessive amounts relative to glucose, is linked to GI distress due to saturation of its specific transporter (GLUT5)[cite: 163, 164]. Unabsorbed fructose can lead to osmotic discomfort[cite: 165, 166]. This underpins our commitment to fructose-free formulations.

üîÑCarbohydrate Absorption Pathways

Glucose (and maltodextrin, a glucose polymer) is absorbed via the SGLT1 transporter, with a capacity of about 60-70 g/hour[cite: 140, 266]. Fructose uses the GLUT5 transporter, with a lower capacity of around 30 g/hour when ingested alone[cite: 142, 268]. P-Formance focuses on optimizing the robust SGLT1 pathway.

üí°Understanding MTC Strategies

The report details the Multiple Transportable Carbohydrate (MTC) strategy (combining glucose and fructose) to potentially increase total carbohydrate oxidation above SGLT1 limits[cite: 132, 174]. While beneficial for some aiming for >90g/hr intakes[cite: 186], P-Formance provides a distinct, high-performance alternative for athletes who prefer to avoid fructose or find their needs met within SGLT1 capacity, minimizing fructose-related risks.

üåøMaltodextrin's Role

The report notes maltodextrin is often preferred in high-concentration products due to lower sweetness and osmolality, which can enhance palatability and GI tolerance[cite: 188, 189, 221]. This aligns with P-Formance's use of optimized maltodextrin/glucose blends.

By understanding these complexities, P-Formance consciously chooses a fructose-free path, aiming to provide the most reliable, gut-friendly, and effective fuel for a significant segment of the athletic population.
